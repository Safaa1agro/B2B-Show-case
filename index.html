I. Allocation of Incoterms (FOB vs. CIF Execution)

Our enterprise supports flexible cross-border structures tailored to importer logistics capabilities. For clients managing their own ocean freight contracts, transactions are executed on a FOB (Free on Board) basis, where our responsibility concludes once cargo is safely loaded past the ship's rail at the origin port. Alternatively, we provide comprehensive CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight) terms, where we secure and fund marine insurance and freight logistics up to the buyer’s designated destination seaport or airport. Under CIF, subsequent customs clearance, local tariff settlement, port handling fees, and domestic cold-chain distribution remain the sole operational and financial liability of the importer.

II. Minimum Order Quantities (MOQ) & Multi-Product Consolidation

To accommodate diverse procurement scopes while strictly safeguarding cold-chain integrity, volume thresholds are structurally optimized by transit mode:

  • Full Container Sea Freight (FCL): Standardized via 20ft and 40ft Reefer/General configurations. To accommodate sub-container volume targets (e.g., a 10,000 kg specific fruit pilot order) without violating thermal isolation rules, we explicitly support Mixed Commodity FCL loading. Importers can consolidate multiple product lines (Meats, Fruits, Vegetables) within a single dedicated container asset to meet full volume capacities.
  • Expedited Air Freight Consolidation (Mixed Cargo Rates): Structured to unlock maximum airline corporate discount rates via a minimum cargo weight threshold of 2,000 kg per dispatch. To provide total purchasing flexibility, we fully support multi-commodity mixing within this 2,000 kg limit. For example, a single routing can consist of 1,000 kg of chilled meat loaded in a premium thermal cargo bucket, paired with 500 kg of fresh tomatoes and 500 kg of green chillies partitioned in separate dry-cargo compartments. This guarantees bulk freight rates while completely avoiding flavor transfer or biological cross-contamination in transit.
  • Buyer-Organized Consolidation (FOB Framework): For single-commodity orders falling below full container capacities where sea freight is required, transactions can be finalized under FOB terms. This allows the importer’s freight forwarder to manage third-party cargo consolidation at the origin port under the buyer's localized risk metrics.

III. Multi-Category Preservation & Quality Protocols

Because our commercial portfolio spans diverse agricultural and livestock profiles, specific preservation metrics apply to safeguard cargo value during transit:

Premium Meats

Chilled cuts are preserved strictly between 0°C to 2°C with absolute humidity control. Deep-frozen bone-in and boneless selections are held unyieldingly at -18°C or colder throughout the voyage.

Fresh Vegetables

Subject to specialized root/leaf temperature curves (typically 4°C to 8°C) paired with optimized ventilation thresholds to counter respiration and preserve structural turgor.

Fresh Fruits

Managed via custom controlled-atmosphere metrics (ranging up to 12°C depending on variety) to rigorously govern ripening deceleration and prevent ethylene buildup during cross-border transit.

IV. Institutional Certification & Compliance Guarantees

Every international consignment leaves our facilities backed by comprehensive commercial clearance sets. All slaughter operations strictly adhere to 100% Halal execution laws, authenticated via authorized international Islamic certification bodies, alongside verified HACCP food safety tracking parameters. Fruit and vegetable exports are subjected to comprehensive sanitary and phytosanitary testing, producing clean Phytosanitary Certificates that ensure strict alignment with international biosecurity frameworks and target destination regulatory authorities (including full SFDA compliance alignment for Middle Eastern entry points).
